"The purpose of existence is the pursuit of enjoyment...in God! Our desires, affections, pursuits, all that we say and do, all that we love or hate, are to be measured by this single criterion and subordinated to this one end: happiness in God." - Sam Storms

"If a man would lead a happy life, let him but seek a sure object for his trust, and he shall be safe: "He shall not be afraid of evil tidings: his heart is fixed, trusting in the Lord." He hath laid up his confidence in God, therefore his heart is kept in an equal poise." - Thomas Manton

"The consideration that human happiness and moral duty are inseparably connected will always continue to prompt me to promote the former by inculcating the practice of the latter." - George Washington

"Jesus makes you happy in reality. The world makes you happy escaping from reality." - Jack Hyles

"There is only one thing greater than happiness in the world, and that is holiness; and it is not in our keeping; but what God HAS put in our power is the happiness of those about us, and that is largely to be secured by our being kind to them." - Henry Drummond

"The world looks for happiness through self-assertion. The Christian knows that joy is found in self-abandonment. 'If a man will let himself be lost for My sake,' Jesus said, 'he will find his true self.'" - Elisabeth Elliot

"Happy are those whose self-centered lives have been crushed and reshaped by the Master's hand to be full of mercy." - John Hagee

"Your success and happiness lie in you. Resolve to keep happy, and your joy and you shall form an invisible host against difficulties." - Helen Keller

"In the destiny of every moral being there is an object more worthy of God than happiness. It is character. And the grand aim of man's creation is the development of a grand character, and grand character is, by its very nature, the product of probationary discipline." - Austin Phelps

"No one has a right to consume happiness without producing it." - Helen Keller

"It is not good to be too free. It is not good to have everything one wants." - Blaise Pascal

"All that we call human history--money, poverty, ambition, war, prostitution, classes, empires, slavery--[is] the long terrible story of man trying to find something other than God which will make him happy." -C.S. Lewis
